Gigs

Saxophonic is for hire! My saxophone duo (soprano and alto) is available for your special occasions. We play Jazz, Classical, Pop, Latin, Christmas favorites and more. Please contact me for your live performance needs. No event is too small, and all the music is good!"

Hijiri Shimamoto

Originally from Okinawa, Japan, Hijiri received her Bachelor of Music degree in Saxophone Performance from the University of North Carolina School of the Arts in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. She studied with Taimur Sullivan and James Kalyn and took master classes with Arno Bornkamp, James Houlik, and the New Century Saxophone Quartet. She has been teaching and performing since 1996 in both Japan and the United States. She was the principal Alto Saxophonist for the Winston-Salem Community Band (N.C., 2005-2012) and the San Jose Metropolitan Band (Calif., 2017-2019), with whom she performed numerous solos. She moved to Santa Fe in March 2019 and has performed regularly with the Santa Fe Concert Band and High Desert Winds. Her new duo, Saxophonic, formed in 2021.

Barbara Gerber

Barbara Gerber is an English teacher, writer, and editor who has been in love with music since she started playing songs by ear on the piano at age five. She began playing saxophone in elementary school and continued through college, where she played in the concert band, jazz ensemble, and saxophone quartet at SUNY College at Buffalo; after transferring to San Francisco State University, she played in its wind ensemble. Barbara moved to Santa Fe in 1987 and eventually joined the Eldorado Community Band in 1999. She then played with Swing Shift Santa Fe, High Desert Winds, and various informal ensembles before joining the Santa Fe Concert Band in 2019. She teamed up with Hijiri Shimamoto in 2021, forming the duo.
