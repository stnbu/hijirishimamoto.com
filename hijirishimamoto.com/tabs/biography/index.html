Biography

Hijiri Shimamoto was born in Okinawa, Japan and received a Bachelor of Music degree in Saxophone Performance from The University of North Carolina School of the Arts in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. She studied with Taimur Sullivan and James Kalyn and took master classes with Arno Bornkamp, James Houlik and The New Century Saxophone Quartet. She has been teaching and performing since 1996 in both Japan and The United States. Hijiri enjoys large ensembles and was the principal Alto Saxophonist for The Winston-Salem Community Band (in NC, 2005-2012) and The San Jose Metropolitan Band (in CA, 2017-2019), with whom she has performed numerous solos. She moved to Santa Fe, New Mexico and joined The Santa Fe Concert Band in April 2019. She has been performing in the Santa Fe area regularly. She also works for Santa Fe Symphony as a music mentor and teaches in the Santa Fe public school system.

She can give lessons in both English and Japanese.
